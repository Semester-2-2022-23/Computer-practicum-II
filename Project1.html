1. Old Town

Koper's Old Town is a labyrinth of narrow cobblestone streets lined with colorful houses, historic buildings, and charming squares. Visitors can wander through the winding alleys, explore hidden courtyards, and discover architectural gems such as the Venetian-style palaces and Gothic churches that dot the area.

2. Koper Castle

Perched on a hill overlooking the city, Koper Castle dates back to the 12th century and offers panoramic views of the Adriatic Sea and the surrounding countryside. The castle now houses the Regional Museum of Koper, where visitors can learn about the city's rich history and cultural heritage through exhibits showcasing archaeological finds, medieval artifacts, and works of art.

3. Praetorian Palace

The Praetorian Palace is a magnificent Renaissance building located in the heart of Koper's Old Town. Built in the 15th century as the seat of the city's Venetian rulers, the palace features a striking façade adorned with elegant arches and decorative motifs. Today, it serves as Koper's city hall and is open to the public for guided tours and special events.

4. Carpaccio Square

Named after the famous Renaissance painter Vittore Carpaccio, Carpaccio Square is a bustling gathering place in Koper's historic center. Surrounded by cafes, restaurants, and shops, the square is a popular spot for locals and visitors alike to relax, people-watch, and soak up the lively atmosphere. At the center of the square stands the imposing Da Ponte Fountain, a symbol of the city's rich artistic heritage.

5. Cathedral of the Assumption

The Cathedral of the Assumption is one of Koper's most important landmarks, renowned for its stunning architecture and rich history. Dating back to the 12th century, the cathedral boasts a magnificent Romanesque-Gothic façade adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures. Inside, visitors can admire beautiful frescoes, Baroque altars, and a magnificent marble pulpit.

6. Muda Gate

The Muda Gate is a well-preserved medieval city gate that once formed part of Koper's defensive walls. Built in the 15th century, the gate features a massive stone archway flanked by imposing towers, offering a glimpse into the city's medieval past. Today, it serves as a reminder of Koper's strategic importance as a trading hub on the Adriatic coast.

7. Da Ponte Fountain

The Da Ponte Fountain is a stunning Renaissance masterpiece located in the heart of Koper's Old Town. Built in the 17th century by the Venetian sculptor Francesco Robba, the fountain features a central marble column adorned with intricate carvings of mythical creatures, sea gods, and allegorical figures. Surrounding the column are four stone dolphins spouting water into a basin below, creating a mesmerizing display of light and sound.

8. Regional Museum of Koper

Housed in a historic Venetian-style palace, the Regional Museum of Koper is dedicated to preserving and showcasing the cultural heritage of the region. The museum's extensive collection includes archaeological artifacts, medieval manuscripts, historical documents, and works of art spanning various periods and styles. Visitors can explore permanent and temporary exhibits that highlight Koper's rich history, from its ancient origins to its role as a vibrant port city.

9. Bell Tower

The Bell Tower is a prominent landmark in Koper's skyline, towering over the city center and offering panoramic views of the surrounding area. Dating back to the 15th century, the tower originally served as a watchtower and defensive fortification. Today, visitors can climb to the top of the tower for sweeping vistas of Koper's rooftops, the Adriatic Sea, and the picturesque Istrian countryside.

10. Koper City Beach

Located just a short walk from the city center, Koper City Beach is a popular destination for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. The sandy beach is equipped with facilities such as showers, changing rooms, and beach bars, making it an ideal spot for a day of relaxation and recreation. Visitors can soak up the sun, take a refreshing dip in the crystal-clear waters of the Adriatic Sea, or enjoy a leisurely stroll along the scenic waterfront promenade.
